# Homework 3: Polynomial Multiplication

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Write all final answers in standard form. When multiplying polynomials, show the intermediate products before combining like terms.

## Part A: Monomial Multiplication

1. Multiply.

- $(3x^2)(4x^5)$
- $(-2x^3)(5x)$
- $(7x)(-x^4)$
- $(6y^2)(3y^3)$

2. Explain the exponent rule used when multiplying $x^m$ and $x^n$.

3. State whether each student answer is correct. If not, fix it.

- $(2x^2)(3x^4)=6x^8$
- $(-5x)(2x^3)=-10x^4$
- $(4a^2)(a^5)=4a^7$

## Part B: Monomial Times Polynomial

4. Multiply and simplify.

- $2x(x^2+3x-5)$
- $-3x^2(2x^3-x+4)$
- $4(5x^2-2x+7)$

5. Multiply and simplify.

- $x(6x^3+2x-1)$
- $-2x(x^2-4x+9)$
- $5x^2(x^2+x+1)$

## Part C: Binomial and Polynomial Products

6. Multiply and simplify.

- $(x+3)(x+8)$
- $(2x-5)(x+4)$
- $(3x+2)(x-6)$

7. Multiply and simplify.

- $(x-7)(x+7)$
- $(x^2+x+1)(x+2)$
- $(2x^2-3x+4)(x-1)$

## Part D: Combine Like Terms Carefully

8. Expand and simplify.

- $(x+1)(x^2+4x+4)$
- $(2x-3)(x^2+x-5)$
- $(3x^2+x)(x+4)$

9. In each problem, some terms cancel after expanding. Simplify completely.

- $(x-5)(x+5)$
- $(2x+1)(x-3)-(2x^2-5x)$
- $(x^2+2x+3)(x-1)$

## Part E: Degree of a Product

10. Without fully multiplying, find the degree of each product.

- $(x^5-2x+1)(3x^2-4)$
- $(2x^3+7)(x^4+x+1)$
- $(x^2-9)(x^2+9)$
- $(4x+1)(x^6-3x^2)$

11. Then fully expand one of the problems from Question 10 to verify your degree answer.

## Part F: Error Analysis and Mixed Practice

12. A student wrote

$$
(x+2)(x+5)=x^2+10
$$

Explain the mistake and find the correct product.

13. A student wrote

$$
3x(x^2-4x+1)=3x^3-12x^2+1
$$

Explain the mistake and find the correct product.

14. Simplify each expression.

- $(x+4)(x-1)$
- $(2x-1)(x^2+3)$
- $(x^2-2x+5)(x+3)$
- $-2x(x^3-x+6)$

15. In your own words, explain why polynomial multiplication is called repeated use of the distributive property.
