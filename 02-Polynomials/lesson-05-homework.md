# Homework 5: Quadratic Standard Form and Factorized Form

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. When using the quadratic formula, substitute the values of $a$, $b$, and $c$ before simplifying. Give exact answers. When asked for factorized form, write the quadratic as a product of linear factors.

## Part A: Roots from Factorized Form

1. In your own words, explain how to find the roots of a quadratic from its factorized form.

2. State the roots of each quadratic.

- $(x-2)(x-7)$
- $4(x+3)(x-1)$
- $(x+5)^2$

3. State the roots of each quadratic.

- $(x-6)(x+4)$
- $2(x-3)^2$
- $(x+1)(x-9)$

## Part B: Factorized Form from Known Roots

4. Write a monic quadratic in factorized form with the given roots.

- $3$ and $-8$
- $5$ and $5$
- $-2$ and $7$

5. Write a quadratic in factorized form with the given roots and leading coefficient.

- roots $4$ and $-1$, leading coefficient $2$
- roots $-3$ and $6$, leading coefficient $5$
- double root $2$, leading coefficient $3$

## Part C: Roots from Standard Form

6. Solve each equation using the quadratic formula.

- $x^2-6x+5=0$
- $x^2-4x-1=0$
- $2x^2+x-3=0$

7. Solve each equation using the quadratic formula.

- $x^2-2x-7=0$
- $3x^2-12x+12=0$
- $x^2+5x+1=0$

## Part D: Factorized Form from Standard Form

8. Write each quadratic in factorized form.

- $x^2-9x+20$
- $x^2-4x-1$
- $x^2+3x-10$

9. Write each quadratic in factorized form.

- $2x^2+x-3$
- $x^2-6x+9$
- $x^2+2x-8$

## Part E: Standard Form from Factorized Form

10. Write each quadratic in standard form.

- $(x-5)(x+2)$
- $2(x+4)(x-1)$
- $(x-3)^2$

11. Write each quadratic in standard form.

- $3(x-2)(x+5)$
- $(x+1)(x-7)$
- $4(x+2)^2$

## Part F: Mixed Practice

12. A quadratic has roots $1+\sqrt{2}$ and $1-\sqrt{2}$. Write its monic factorized form.

13. A quadratic is given by

$$
f(x)=x^2-8x+15.
$$

Find its roots and then write its factorized form.

14. A student says, "The roots of $(x-4)(x+6)$ are $-4$ and $6$." Explain the mistake and give the correct roots.

15. In your own words, explain how standard form and factorized form describe the same quadratic in different ways.
