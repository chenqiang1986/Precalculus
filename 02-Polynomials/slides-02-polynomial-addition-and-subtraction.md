# Lesson 2
## Polynomial Addition and Subtraction

Precalculus

---

# Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

- identify like terms in a polynomial expression;
- explain why only like terms can be combined;
- add polynomials correctly;
- subtract polynomials correctly;
- use standard form to organize work;
- simplify a polynomial sum or difference and state its degree.

---

# Big Idea

Adding and subtracting polynomials is really about one main skill:

- combine **like terms**;
- keep **unlike terms** separate.

Standard form makes this much easier to see.

---

# Knowledge Point 1

**Like terms** have the same variable part.

That means:

- the same variable;
- the same exponent.

Examples of like terms:

$$
3x^2,\ -5x^2,\ \frac{1}{2}x^2
$$

Examples of unlike terms:

$$
4x^3 \text{ and } 4x^2,\qquad 2x \text{ and } 2
$$

---

# Why This Matters

These can be combined:

$$
3x^2+5x^2=8x^2
$$

These cannot be combined:

$$
3x^2+5x
$$

because $x^2$ and $x$ are different powers.

# Knowledge Point 2

To **add polynomials**:

1. remove parentheses if needed;
2. group like terms;
3. combine coefficients;
4. write the result in standard form.

Example:

$$
(3x^2+2x-5)+(x^2-7x+4)
$$

---

# Adding Example

$$
(3x^2+2x-5)+(x^2-7x+4)
$$

Group like terms:

$$
(3x^2+x^2)+(2x-7x)+(-5+4)
$$

Combine:

$$
4x^2-5x-1
$$

---

2. $(4x^3-2x)+(x^3+7x-6)$
3. $(8x-9)+(3x^2-2x+5)$

Write each answer in standard form.

---

# Knowledge Point 3

Standard form helps us line up matching powers.

Example:

$$
(5x^3-2x+7)+(x^2+4x-9)
$$

Rewrite mentally as:

$$
5x^3+0x^2-2x+7
$$

and

$$
0x^3+x^2+4x-9
$$

Missing powers can be treated as having coefficient $0$.

---

# Vertical Method

You can also align terms vertically.

$$
\begin{aligned}
&5x^3+0x^2-2x+7\\
+{}&0x^3+x^2+4x-9
\end{aligned}
$$

Then combine each column:

$$
5x^3+x^2+2x-2
$$

---

2. $(x^5+4x^2-1)+(3x^3-4x^2+9)$
3. $(7-2x^2)+(5x^2+x-6)$

Look carefully for missing powers.

---

# Knowledge Point 4

To **subtract polynomials**, distribute the minus sign to every term in the second polynomial.

Example:

$$
(4x^2-3x+1)-(x^2+5x-6)
$$

becomes

$$
4x^2-3x+1-x^2-5x+6
$$

Then combine like terms.

---

# Subtraction Example

Start with

$$
(4x^2-3x+1)-(x^2+5x-6)
$$

Distribute the negative:

$$
4x^2-3x+1-x^2-5x+6
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
3x^2-8x+7
$$

---

# Common Mistake

This is wrong:

$$
(4x^2-3x+1)-(x^2+5x-6)=4x^2-3x+1-x^2+5x-6
$$

Why?

- the minus sign must change **every** term in the second polynomial;
- $+5x$ should become $-5x$;
- $-6$ should become $+6$.

---

---

# Knowledge Point 5

After simplifying, the result is still a polynomial.

Then we can find its degree from the final answer.

Important:

- the degree comes from the **simplified result**;
- leading terms can cancel.

Example:

$$
(3x^3+2x)-(3x^3-x^2+x)=x^2+x
$$

The degree is $2$, not $3$.

---

# Cancellation Example

Consider

$$
(5x^2-1)+(-5x^2+4x+6)
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
5x^2-5x^2+4x-1+6=4x+5
$$

The $x^2$ terms cancel, so the degree drops to $1$.

# Knowledge Point 6

A good checking habit:

- first check the signs;
- then check that only like terms were combined;
- finally check that the answer is in standard form.

This catches many subtraction mistakes.

---

# Summary

Today we learned:

- like terms must have the same variable and the same exponent;
- polynomial addition means combining like terms;
- polynomial subtraction means changing the signs of the second polynomial first;
- standard form helps organize the work;
- the degree is found from the simplified final result.
