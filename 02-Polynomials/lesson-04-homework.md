# Homework 4: Polynomial Long Division

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Write all polynomials in standard form before dividing. If powers are missing, include zero coefficients as placeholders. For each long division problem, show the divide, multiply, subtract, and bring down steps.

## Part A: Set Up the Division

1. Rewrite each polynomial in standard form with missing powers included.

- $x^3-5x+2$
- $2x^4+x^2-7$
- $x^5+3x^2-1$
- $4x^3+7$

2. Explain why missing powers matter in polynomial long division.

3. Without completing the full division, state the first term of the quotient for each.

- $\dfrac{x^2+6x+8}{x+2}$
- $\dfrac{2x^3-x+5}{x-1}$
- $\dfrac{x^4+3x^2-1}{x^2+1}$

## Part B: Exact Division

4. Divide and simplify.

- $\dfrac{x^2+7x+10}{x+5}$
- $\dfrac{x^2-x-12}{x-4}$
- $\dfrac{2x^2+9x+9}{x+3}$

5. Divide and simplify.

- $\dfrac{x^3+x^2-2x-2}{x+1}$
- $\dfrac{x^3-3x^2-4x+12}{x-3}$
- $\dfrac{2x^3-3x^2-8x+12}{x+2}$

## Part C: Quotient and Remainder

6. Divide. State the quotient and remainder.

- $\dfrac{x^3-4x+1}{x-2}$
- $\dfrac{2x^3-5x^2+4x-1}{x-2}$
- $\dfrac{x^3+2x^2-5}{x+2}$

7. Divide. State the quotient and remainder.

- $\dfrac{x^3+4x^2+x+7}{x+3}$
- $\dfrac{3x^3+x^2-6x+5}{x-1}$
- $\dfrac{x^4-1}{x+1}$

## Part D: Check by Multiplying Back

8. Check your result in problem 7 matching statement in the form

$$
\text{dividend}=(\text{divisor})(\text{quotient})+\text{remainder}
$$

9. Decide whether each student answer is correct. If not, correct it.

- $\dfrac{x^2+5x+6}{x+2}=x+1$ with remainder $4$
- $\dfrac{x^3-4x+1}{x-2}=x^2-2$ with remainder $1$
- $\dfrac{2x^3-3x^2-8x+12}{x+2}=2x^2-7x+6$
