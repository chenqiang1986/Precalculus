# Homework 6: Vieta's Theorem

## Instructions

Do not solve for the roots unless a problem explicitly asks you to. Use Vieta's theorem and algebraic rewriting to answer each question from the coefficients.

## Part A: Sum and Product of the Roots

1. State Vieta's theorem for the quadratic equation

$$
ax^2+bx+c=0.
$$

2. Without solving the equation, find the sum and product of the roots.

- $x^2-9x+14=0$
- $2x^2+x-6=0$
- $3x^2-5x-4=0$

3. Without solving the equation, find the sum and product of the roots.

- $x^2+6x+1=0$
- $4x^2-12x+7=0$
- $5x^2-20=0$

## Part B: Reciprocal Expressions

4. Without solving for the roots, find

$$
\frac{1}{r_1}+\frac{1}{r_2}
$$

for each equation.

- $x^2-5x+6=0$
- $x^2+4x-5=0$
- $2x^2-7x+3=0$

5. Without solving for the roots, decide whether

$$
\frac{1}{r_1}+\frac{1}{r_2}
$$

can be found directly. If yes, find it. If no, explain why.

- $x^2-3x=0$
- $2x^2+x-1=0$
- $x^2-8x+16=0$

## Part C: Squares of the Roots

6. Without solving for the roots, find

$$
r_1^2+r_2^2
$$

for each equation.

- $x^2-6x+7=0$
- $x^2+2x-3=0$
- $2x^2-3x+4=0$

7. Without solving for the roots, find

$$
(r_1-r_2)^2
$$

for each equation.

- $x^2-5x+3=0$
- $x^2+x-1=0$
- $3x^2-6x+2=0$

## Part D: Mixed Root Expressions

8. Without solving for the roots, find

$$
\frac{r_1}{r_2}+\frac{r_2}{r_1}
$$

for each equation.

- $x^2-3x+1=0$
- $x^2-4x-1=0$
- $2x^2-5x+2=0$

9. For the equation $x^2-7x+10=0$, find each quantity without solving for the roots.

- $r_1+r_2$
- $r_1r_2$
- $\dfrac{1}{r_1}+\dfrac{1}{r_2}$
- $r_1^2+r_2^2$

## Part E: Reasoning and Error Analysis

10. A student starts solving $x^2-8x+15=0$ with the quadratic formula to find $r_1+r_2$. Explain a faster method.

11. A student says, "For $x^2-4x=0$, the value of $\dfrac{1}{r_1}+\dfrac{1}{r_2}$ is $4$." Explain the mistake.

12. In your own words, explain why Vieta's theorem is useful even when you never compute the actual roots.
