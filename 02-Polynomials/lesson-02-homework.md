# Homework 2: Polynomial Addition and Subtraction

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Write final answers in standard form. For subtraction problems, show the step where you distribute the negative sign.

## Part A: Like Terms

1. In your own words, explain what makes two terms like terms.

2. From each list, group the like terms together.

- $4x^2,\ -3x,\ 7x^2,\ 9,\ -2$
- $5x^3,\ x,\ -8x^3,\ 6x,\ 1$
- $2y^2,\ -4y,\ 3,\ 9y^2,\ -7$

3. State whether each pair is like terms or unlike terms.

- $6x^4$ and $-2x^4$
- $3x^2$ and $3x$
- $8$ and $-5$
- $7a$ and $7a^2$

## Part B: Polynomial Addition

4. Add and simplify.

- $(3x^2+5x-1)+(2x^2-x+4)$
- $(4x^3-7x+6)+(x^3+2x-9)$
- $(8-3x)+(5x^2+x-2)$

5. Add and simplify.

- $(6x^4+x^2-5)+(3x^2+2x+1)$
- $(x^5-4x+7)+(2x^3+9)$
- $(9x^2-6)+(5-2x^2+x)$

## Part C: Polynomial Subtraction

6. Subtract and simplify.

- $(7x^2-3x+8)-(2x^2+x-5)$
- $(5x^3+4x-1)-(x^3-6x+2)$
- $(10-2x^2)-(x^2+3x-4)$

7. Subtract and simplify.

- $(8x^4-x+6)-(3x^4+2x-1)$
- $(2x^5+7)-(2x^5-3x^2+4)$
- $(4x^2-9x+3)-(6x^2-2x-8)$

## Part D: Simplify and State the Degree

8. Simplify each expression and state the degree of the result.

- $(2x^3+x^2-4)+(5x^3-3x^2+1)$
- $(9x^4-2x+6)-(4x^4+7x-3)$
- $(3x^2-5)+(-3x^2+x+8)$
- $(6x^5+x^2)-(6x^5-x^3+4)$

9. In each problem, the leading terms cancel. Simplify and state the degree.

- $(4x^3+2x^2-1)+(-4x^3+5x-6)$
- $(7x^2-x+9)-(7x^2+3x-2)$
- $(5x^4+1)-(5x^4-2x^2+8)$

## Part E: Error Analysis and Mixed Practice

10. A student wrote

$$
(6x^2-3x+1)-(2x^2+4x-5)=4x^2+x-4
$$

Is the student correct? Explain and find the correct answer.

11. A student wrote

$$
(3x^2+2x)+(5x+1)=3x^2+7x+1
$$

Is the student correct? Explain why or why not.

12. Simplify each expression.

- $(x^3-2x^2+x-4)+(4x^2-3x+9)$
- $(5x^4+x^2-7)-(2x^4-6x+1)$
- $(8x-3)+(-5x+11)$
- $(9x^2-4x+6)+(-9x^2+4x-6)$

13. Explain why standard form makes polynomial addition and subtraction easier.

14. One student says, "When subtracting polynomials, only the first sign changes." Is this correct? Explain with an example.

15. Write your own two polynomials whose sum has degree $1$. Then add them and show why the degree of the result is $1$.
