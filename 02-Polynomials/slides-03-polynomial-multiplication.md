# Lesson 3
## Polynomial Multiplication

Precalculus

---

# Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

- multiply monomials correctly;
- use the distributive property to multiply a monomial by a polynomial;
- multiply two polynomials term by term;
- combine like terms in a product;
- write products in standard form;
- find the degree of a product of nonzero polynomials.

---

# Big Idea

Polynomial multiplication is built on one main rule:

- multiply **every term** in one factor by **every term** in the other factor.

Then combine like terms and write the result in standard form.

---

# Knowledge Point 1

To multiply monomials:

1. multiply the coefficients;
2. add the exponents of matching variables.

Example:

$$
(3x^2)(-2x^5)=-6x^7
$$

because $3\cdot(-2)=-6$ and $x^2\cdot x^5=x^7$.

---

# Exponent Reminder

When multiplying powers with the same base:

$$
x^m\cdot x^n=x^{m+n}
$$

This is multiplication, not addition.

For example:

$$
x^2\cdot x^3=x^5
$$

not $x^6$.

# Knowledge Point 2

To multiply a monomial by a polynomial, use the distributive property.

Example:

$$
3x(x^2-4x+5)
$$

Multiply $3x$ by each term:

$$
3x^3-12x^2+15x
$$

---

# One-Term by Many-Term

Think of

$$
3x(x^2-4x+5)
$$

as:

- $3x\cdot x^2$
- $3x\cdot(-4x)$
- $3x\cdot 5$

Every term inside the parentheses gets multiplied.

---

# Class Practice 2

1. $2x(3x^2+x-4)$
2. $-4x^2(x^3-2x+1)$
3. $5(2x^2-7x+6)$

Write each answer in standard form.

---

# Knowledge Point 3

To multiply two polynomials, distribute each term in the first polynomial across the second.

Example:

$$
(x+3)(x+5)
$$

Multiply term by term:

$$
x(x+5)+3(x+5)
$$

Then simplify.

---

# Binomial Times Binomial

$$
(x+3)(x+5)
$$

Multiply:

$$
x^2+5x+3x+15
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
x^2+8x+15
$$

---

# Class Practice 3

1. $(x+4)(x+2)$
2. $(2x-3)(x+5)$
3. $(3x+1)(x-4)$

---

# Knowledge Point 4

The same idea works for larger polynomials.

Example:

$$
(x^2+x+1)(x+2)
$$

Multiply each term in $x^2+x+1$ by each term in $x+2$.

---

# Larger Product Example

$$
(x^2+x+1)(x+2)
$$

Distribute:

$$
x^2(x+2)+x(x+2)+1(x+2)
$$

Expand:

$$
x^3+2x^2+x^2+2x+x+2
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
x^3+3x^2+3x+2
$$

---

# Organizing the Work

You can organize multiplication in rows:

$$
\begin{aligned}
(x^2+x+1)(x+2)
\end{aligned}
$$

Row 1 from multiplying by $x$:

$$
x^3+x^2+x
$$

Row 2 from multiplying by $2$:

$$
2x^2+2x+2
$$

Then add the rows.

---

# Knowledge Point 5

After multiplying, always:

1. combine like terms;
2. write the answer in standard form;
3. check whether any terms cancel.

Example:

$$
(x-2)(x+2)=x^2+2x-2x-4=x^2-4
$$

The middle terms cancel.

# Knowledge Point 6

For nonzero polynomials:

$$
\deg(PQ)=\deg(P)+\deg(Q)
$$

Example:

- if $\deg(P)=2$ and $\deg(Q)=3$,
- then $\deg(PQ)=5$.

This comes from multiplying the leading terms.

---

# Degree Example

Consider

$$
(2x^3-x+1)(-4x^2+5)
$$

The leading terms are:

$$
2x^3 \quad \text{and} \quad -4x^2
$$

Their product is

$$
-8x^5
$$

So the product has degree $5$.

# Summary

Today we learned:

- multiply monomials by multiplying coefficients and adding exponents of matching variables;
- multiply a monomial across every term in a polynomial;
- multiply two polynomials by distributing every term to every term;
- combine like terms after expanding;
- the degree of a product of nonzero polynomials is the sum of the degrees.
