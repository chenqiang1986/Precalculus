# Homework 7: General Polynomial in Factorized Form

## Instructions

Work directly from factorized form whenever possible. For inequalities, use a sign chart or sign reasoning on intervals. For rough sketches, clearly label roots, multiplicities, and end behavior.

## Part A: Roots, Multiplicity, and Degree

1. In your own words, explain what the multiplicity of a root means.

2. For each polynomial, list the roots, multiplicities, and degree.

- $(x-2)^3(x+1)$
- $-2(x+4)^2(x-5)^3$
- $5(x-1)^4$

3. For each polynomial, say whether the graph crosses or touches the $x$-axis at each root.

- $(x+3)^2(x-1)$
- $(x-4)^3(x+2)^2$
- $-(x+1)^4(x-6)$

## Part B: Solving Polynomial Inequalities

4. Solve each inequality.

- $(x-2)(x+3)>0$
- $(x+1)^2(x-4)>0$
- $-(x-5)(x+2)^2>0$

5. Solve each inequality.

- $(x-3)^2(x+1)<0$
- $-(x+4)(x-2)<0$
- $(x+2)^3(x-1)<0$

6. For

$$
f(x)=(x+3)^2(x-1)(x-4),
$$

determine the sign of $f(x)$ on each interval cut by the roots, then solve:

- $f(x)>0$
- $f(x)<0$

## Part C: Reading Graph Features from Factorized Form

7. For each polynomial, state:

- the degree;
- the sign of the leading coefficient;
- the end behavior;
- the $x$-intercepts with multiplicity;
- the $y$-intercept.

- $(x+2)(x-1)^2$
- $-(x-3)^3(x+1)$
- $2(x+4)^2(x-2)^2$

8. For each polynomial in Problem 7, state whether the graph crosses or touches at each root, then sketch a rough graph.

## Part D: Mixed Reasoning

9. A student says, "Since $x=2$ is a root of multiplicity $2$, the graph crosses the $x$-axis twice at $x=2$." Explain the mistake.

10. Write a polynomial in factorized form with:

- roots $-1$ and $3$;
- multiplicities $2$ and $1$;
- leading coefficient $-2$.

Then state:

- the degree;
- the end behavior;
- whether the graph crosses or touches at each root.

11. For

$$
f(x)=-(x+2)^2(x-1)(x-5),
$$

describe a rough sketch in words. Include end behavior, intercept behavior, and where the graph is above or below the $x$-axis.
