# Homework 2: Composition and Inverse Functions

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Write answers using function notation whenever possible.

## Part A: Function Composition

1. Let:

$$
f(x) = 3x + 2
$$

$$
g(x) = x - 5
$$

Find:

- $(f \circ g)(x)$
- $(g \circ f)(x)$

2. Let:

$$
f(x) = x^2
$$

$$
g(x) = 2x + 1
$$

Find $(f \circ g)(2)$ and $(g \circ f)(2)$.

3. Suppose the raw mappings are:

$$
g: 1 \to 2,\quad 2 \to 3,\quad 3 \to 1
$$

$$
f: 1 \to 4,\quad 2 \to 5,\quad 3 \to 6
$$

Find:

- $g(3)$
- $f(2)$
- $(f \circ g)(1)$
- $(f \circ g)(3)$

4. In one or two sentences, explain why order matters in composition.

## Part B: Finding Inverses

5. Find the inverse of:

$$
f(x) = 2x - 7
$$

6. Find the inverse of:

$$
g(x) = \frac{x+5}{4}
$$

7. Check your answer to Problem 5 by showing that $f(f^{-1}(x)) = x$.

## Part C: One-to-One Functions

8. Explain why $f(x) = x^2$ does not have an inverse on all real numbers.

9. State a restriction on the domain of $f(x) = x^2$ that allows an inverse to exist.

10. Does $f(x) = x^3$ have an inverse on all real numbers? Explain briefly.

## Part D: Graph Thinking

11. Which test helps determine whether a function has an inverse?

12. Would the graph of a linear function with nonzero slope pass the horizontal line test? Explain briefly.

13. Give an example of a function that fails the horizontal line test.

## Challenge

14. Suppose a function satisfies

$$
f(1)=4,\qquad f(2)=7,\qquad f(3)=10
$$

Write the matching inverse relation using ordered pairs.

## Optional Reflection

15. In 3 to 5 sentences, explain the difference between function composition and an inverse function.
