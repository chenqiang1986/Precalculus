# Homework 2: Composition and Inverse Functions

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Write answers using function notation whenever possible.

## Part A: Function Composition

1. Let

$$
f(x)=-2x+5
$$

and

$$
g(x)=x+3
$$

Find:

- $(f \circ g)(x)$
- $(g \circ f)(x)$

2. Let

$$
p(x)=x^2-4
$$

and

$$
q(x)=x-1
$$

Find:

- $(p \circ q)(3)$
- $(q \circ p)(3)$

3. Suppose the raw mappings are

$$
m: 1 \to 4,\quad 2 \to 1,\quad 3 \to 2,\quad 4 \to 3
$$

and

$$
n: 1 \to 6,\quad 2 \to 8,\quad 3 \to 10,\quad 4 \to 12
$$

Find:

- $m(4)$
- $n(2)$
- $(n \circ m)(1)$
- $(n \circ m)(3)$

4. In one or two sentences, explain why order matters in composition. Use one example from Problems 1 or 2.

## Part B: Finding Inverses

5. Find the inverse of

$$
f(x)=-4x+6
$$

6. Find the inverse of

$$
g(x)=\frac{2x-3}{5}
$$

7. Check your answer to Problem 5 by showing that both

$$
f(f^{-1}(x))=x
$$

and

$$
f^{-1}(f(x))=x
$$

## Part C: One-to-One Functions

8. Explain why

$$
h(x)=(x-2)^2
$$

does not have an inverse on all real numbers.

9. Restrict the domain of

$$
h(x)=(x-2)^2
$$

to

$$
x \ge 2
$$

Then find the inverse.

10. Now restrict the domain of the same function to

$$
x \le 2
$$

What should the inverse be?

11. Does

$$
r(x)=x^3-1
$$

have an inverse on all real numbers? Explain briefly.

## Part D: Graph Thinking

12. Which test helps determine whether a function has an inverse?

13. Would the graph of

$$
y=-3x+2
$$

pass the horizontal line test? Explain briefly.

14. Give an example of a function that fails the horizontal line test, and explain why it fails.

## Challenge

15. Suppose a function satisfies

$$
f(2)=1,\qquad f(5)=4,\qquad f(8)=7
$$

Write the matching inverse relation using ordered pairs.
