# Homework 1: Introduction to Functions

## Instructions

Show your work clearly. Use function notation whenever possible.

## Part A: Function Basics

1. Decide whether each relation is a function. Briefly explain your answer.

   a. $1 \to 4, 2\to 5, 3 \to 6$

   b. $0 \to 2, 0 \to 3, 1 \to 5$

   c. $-2 \to 1,\ 0 \to 1,\ 3 \to 4$

2. For the function

$$
f:\{-1,0,2\}\to\{1,3,7,9\}
$$

defined by

$$
-1\to1,\quad 0\to3,\quad 2\to7
$$

find:

- the domain
- the codomain
- the range

3. Explain why two different inputs are allowed to have the same output in a function.

## Part B: Tables and Raw Mappings

4. Use the table below to answer the questions.

| x | -3 | -1 | 0 | 2 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| f(x) | 9 | 1 | 0 | 4 | 16 |

- What is $f(-1)$?
- What is $f(2)$?
- Which input gives output $16$?
- Do two different inputs give the same output in this table?

5. For the table in Problem 4, state:

- the domain
- the codomain, using only the outputs shown in the table
- the range

6. Consider the mapping

$$
h:\{2,4,6\}\to\{0,1,4,9\}
$$

given by

$$
2\to0,\quad 4\to4,\quad 6\to9
$$

Find the domain, codomain, and range.

## Part C: Functions Given by Expressions

7. Let

$$
p(x)=2x^2+1
$$

find:

- $p(0)$
- $p(2)$
- $p(-1)$

8. Consider

$$
f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R},\qquad f(x)=x^2+4
$$

Find the domain, codomain, and range.

9. Consider

$$
g:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R},\qquad g(x)=x-3
$$

Find the domain, codomain, and range. Then state whether the codomain and range are the same.

10. Now consider the same rule

$$
g(x)=x-3
$$

but with

$$
g:[0,4]\to\mathbb{R}
$$

Find the domain, codomain, and range.

## Part D: Default Domain and Range

11. If a domain is not stated explicitly, what domain do we usually assume?

12. Find the domain and range of

$$
f(x)=\frac{5}{x+1}
$$

13. Find the domain and range of

$$
g(x)=\sqrt{3x-6}
$$

14. Find the domain of

$$
h(x)=\frac{\sqrt{x+4}}{x-1}
$$

15. For the function in Problem 14, describe what conditions the input must satisfy.

## Part E: Piecewise Functions

16. Let

$$
f(x)=
\begin{cases}
2x-1, & x<2 \\
x+3, & x\ge 2
\end{cases}
$$

find:

- $f(-2)$
- $f(2)$
- $f(3)$

17. Find the domain and range of

$$
f(x)=
\begin{cases}
x^2, & x<0 \\
1-x, & x\ge 0
\end{cases}
$$

18. Find the domain and range of

$$
g(x)=
\begin{cases}
\sqrt{x+1}, & -1\le x<3 \\
2x-5, & x\ge 3
\end{cases}
$$

## Part F: Function Graphs

19. Use Desmos or a hand sketch to look at the graph of

$$
f(x)=-x+4
$$

Describe its shape and where it crosses the $y$-axis.

20. Use Desmos or a hand sketch to look at the graph of

$$
g(x)=(x-1)^2
$$

Describe its shape and its lowest point.

21. Use Desmos or a hand sketch to look at the graph of

$$
h(x)=
\begin{cases}
1-x, & x<1 \\
x^2-1, & x\ge 1
\end{cases}
$$

Describe how the two pieces fit together.

22. What test tells whether a graph represents a function?

## Challenge

23. Create your own function with:

- domain $\{1,2,3,4\}$
- codomain $\{2,4,6,8,10\}$
- range $\{2,6,10\}$

Write it as a mapping or a table.

## Optional Reflection

24. In 3 to 5 sentences, explain the difference between domain, codomain, and range.
